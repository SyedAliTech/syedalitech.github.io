Albany: The Capital City

Albany is the capital city of New York State. With a population of around 100,000, it serves as the political and cultural hub of the state. Albany was incorporated as a city in 1686 and has a long history dating back to the colonial era.

It is classified as an urban area and is situated in the eastern region of the state. The city's average income level is reflective of the state's economic landscape.

Quick Facts

  • Population: 100,000+
  • Year Incorporated: 1686
  • Region: Eastern New York
  • Classification: Urban
